Strabo Privacy Policy:

Last updated: 04 October 2020


About Us:

Strabo Financial Ltd is a company registered in England & Wales (registration number: 12585284), registered at 27 Old Gloucester Street, London, WC1N3AX ("Strabo", "we", "us", "our").

At Strabo, data protection and privacy are of great importance to us. We commit to provide clear information on how and why we store and process your personal data. You can contact us via our email address hello@strabo.app or get in touch through our social media accounts.


What Personal Data do we store and use?

When you browse our website:
When you browse our website (www.strabo.app) we only collect your IP address for security purposes. You can find more information in the Cookies Policy below.

When you contact us:
When you contact us through email or our social media accounts, we store your personal information to provide you with prompt feedback regarding your queries.

When you use our services:
We are making our services available to those individuals who sign up for the beta version of our web-based Dashboard. At Strabo, we are connecting accounts across the globe, starting off with a limited number of countries.

You can enroll to use Strabo's services by clicking on "Request Access" on our website. We collect your first and last name, email address, current country of residence and countries where you have financial accounts. We need this information to make our services available to you, and prioritise the availability of our services to all our users based on their location.

The Strabo Dashboard does not require, and hence store, login credentials like usernames and passwords. This is managed through trusted third-party authentication from either Google or Apple, as of the date of this policy.

To provide our services on the Strabo Dashboard, we use Open Banking implemented by Plaid , which is a trusted third-party Account Information Service Provider (AISP) under the Revised Payments Service Directive. Plaid is our only Open Banking partner as of the date of this policy.

For the provision of our services through our Dashboard, we store and process personal and financial information


Consent:

At Strabo, we will always ask for your consent before we store and process your personal data. If, however, you change your mind in the future, we can always contact us to withdraw your consent. The terms and conditions of our service can be found here: https://www.strabo.app/terms.html

How do we store your Data? At Strabo, we strive to keep adequate measures in place, all physical, technical and operational to protect your data from unauthorised access, use and disclosure. We have deployed our technology using secure cloud computing services from Amazon Web Services & Google. We have strict guidelines for Strabo personnel with access to your data complying with GDPR best practises.

Who do we share your data with? To help us provide our services, we share encrypted and as little data as possible with third-parties so you get the best experience out of Strabo. This may include our Open Banking Partner, Cloud computing companies, software companies for email marketing, marketing automation, customer service, and business analytics. We will always enrue all the right checks and balances are in place before your data is shared with any third party.

Your Rights: At Strabo, you always come first. You can, at any time, ask us to provide any data we have stored about you via email at hello@strabo.app. You can reach out to us to correct any inaccurate data we hold about you. And you always have the right to withdraw consent you have given us.

Please let us know whenever you have a complaint regarding Strabo & your personal data, we will make our best efforts to rectify it. Should you not feel satisfied you have the right to file an official complaint with the ICO.

Changes to this policy:

We may make changes to this policy from time to any to align with any changes to the service or legal requirements, and all such changes will be posted here. The date of this policy states the time of the last amendment to the policy.
